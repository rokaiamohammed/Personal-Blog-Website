Benefits of building puzzles

Author

Rokaia Mohamed
Engineer and Web developer
Oct 14 2023

Here are some surprising benefits of building puzzles that we'd like to share with you!

1. Improved memory

Building puzzles can help improve short-term memory and mental faculties by helping you remember shapes, details, and colors

2. Better spatial reasoning

To put together a puzzle, we have to look at a mass of small pieces and place them within a larger image. Doing this exercise regularly helps us improve our spatial awareness.

3. Better productivity

Puzzles can help sharpen your attention to detail as well as your concentration, which can lead to better productivity

4. Improve your memory

The simple act of assembling a puzzle works to reinforce the connections between our brain cells. This is great for our short-term memory!

5. Lower stress levels

While putting puzzles together, your brain tends to move from “beta” waves, or a more awake mode, to “alpha” waves , similar to the state you're in when dreaming. Moving from beta to alpha nets you benefits like lower blood pressure, lowered heart rate, and increased self-confidence as well as a better mood

6. Improve your problem-solving ability

Puzzles can help boost problem-solving skills, hand-eye coordination, and critical thinking. Puzzles can also help with memory retention, spatial awareness, and overall cognitive development

7. Sense of accomplishment

Completing a puzzle provides an incredible sense of confidence-building accomplishment. It can be a great way to unwind and relax, and can even lower blood pressure and reduce anxiety

8. Improve your mood

Solving puzzles has a great benefit: it increases brain production of dopamine. This neurotransmitter is responsible for regulating mood and optimism. It also affects learning, memory, concentration, and motivation. Dopamine is released every time we do a puzzle and even every time we put a piece in the right place. This encourages us to continue to do so and challenge ourselves.
